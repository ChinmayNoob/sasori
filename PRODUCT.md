# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Knowledge workers in focused work sessions: researchers digging through reports, analysts cross-referencing data, PMs pulling action items from meeting notes. They have a specific question, a deadline, and no patience for friction. They use Sasori as a tool, not a destination. Success is getting an accurate, cited answer in seconds and moving on.

## Product Purpose

Sasori syncs Google Drive documents, indexes them with vector embeddings, and lets users ask questions in natural language. A ReAct agent reasons through queries step by step, searches the indexed knowledge base and the web, and returns answers with clickable source citations. The product exists to make organizational knowledge instantly accessible and trustworthy.

## Brand Personality

Ethereal, refined, understated. Warm stone and pearl tones. Serif headings with editorial weight. The interface should feel like a calm, confident assistant, not a flashy tech product. Thin weights, generous whitespace, material warmth over cold chrome.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI chat interfaces: dark sidebars, message bubbles in tech-blue, ChatGPT-clone layouts with the same conversational chrome.
- SaaS landing templates: hero-metric sections (big number, small label, gradient accent), identical card grids with icon + heading + text, gradient CTAs, the full Formula.
- Any interface where someone could guess the palette from the category alone ("AI tool = dark blue").

## Design Principles

1. **Calm confidence.** The interface is composed and unhurried. It does not shout, wiggle, or beg for attention. Trust comes from restraint.
2. **Transparent reasoning.** The agent's thinking is visible in real time. Users see the plan, the steps, the reasoning. No black box.
3. **Earned density.** Information appears when needed. The empty state is spacious; the active state fills naturally. Never overwhelm upfront.
4. **Material warmth.** Warm neutrals, serif typography, soft shadows over hard edges. The interface feels tactile, not digital-first.
5. **Citations as first class.** Every answer is grounded in source documents. Citations are not an afterthought or footer detail; they are the proof that makes the answer trustworthy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Basic semantic HTML and reasonable contrast. No formal WCAG target yet. Accessibility will be addressed as the product matures.
